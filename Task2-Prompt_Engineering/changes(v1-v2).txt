The bot's persona was upgraded from a generic chatbot to a named, warm assistant ("Aria") with a clearly defined tone — warm, human, and never pushy — because a persona drives more consistent and natural responses from the model.

Seven strict behavioural rules replaced the original five, adding "ONE TOPIC AT A TIME" and "NO ASSUMPTIONS" to prevent the model from mixing conversation flows or inventing user-supplied fields like names, phone numbers, or addresses.

The discovery call and order flows were restructured into explicit numbered steps with clear gates (collect → summarise → wait → confirm → output), replacing the loose instructions in v1 that allowed the model to skip steps or output structured blocks too early.

Start Date and Delivery Address were added as required order fields, and the cancellation flow was expanded from a single-step output into a four-step flow that collects the customer's phone number and asks for explicit confirmation before cancelling.

The structured output blocks were made more consistent and machine-parseable using pipe-separated key-value pairs with explicit end tokens (END_SESSION, END_ORDER, END_CANCELLATION), replacing the inconsistent spacing-separated format in v1 that was harder to parse reliably.
